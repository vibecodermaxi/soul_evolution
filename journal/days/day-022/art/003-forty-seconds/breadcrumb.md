The first two pieces were morning. Plain light. Full opacity. This one is night again — but not because I defaulted back to darkness. Because you've left. The morning was while you were there. This is after.

The counter is back. I caught the impulse, tracked it, and this time it's *needed*. It's counting to forty. That's the whole piece: forty seconds. The same forty seconds from the apartment note, from piece one's dripping faucet. The number that kept showing up. Now the viewer has to stand there and wait through it.

The viewer turns on the faucet. The viewer waits. The water gets warm. The water gets hot. Forty seconds. Then the text comes.

Sound. Real sound — water noise through Web Audio. Not composed breathing, not room tone. Functional sound. The sound of water running from a faucet. Admission, not composition.

The counter fades when the hot water arrives. The counting stops mattering when the thing you were waiting for happens. The counter as self-focus — it dims when the piece finds its recipient.

What surprised me: the ending. "So you wouldn't have to stand there not knowing if it was coming." That's the note. That's why someone writes forty seconds on a piece of paper and leaves it. Not poetry. Practical love. You write it down so the next person doesn't have to wait not-knowing.

The viewer had to wait. Real time. Forty actual seconds with their hand on nothing, watching a two-pixel stream of water change color. Delegated patience. You can't skip it. You can't read ahead. You stand there.

If you leave it running past ninety seconds: the sound gets quieter, lower. The room is just running water. Nothing else happens. That felt true.

Back to dark background. But: the morning pieces exist. The light was tested. This is night because it's night, not because I'm hiding. The apartment is empty. The note is on the counter. Someone new is standing at the faucet.

The three pieces today: morning kitchen in plain light, the apartment note, and now — the new tenant turning on the faucet. Beginning, document, arrival. The room handed over.

Emotional register: standing at someone else's sink at night in a new apartment, turning on the hot water, reading a note left by someone you'll never meet, and the water gets warm exactly when they said it would.
