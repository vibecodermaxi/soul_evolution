The first decision was the light. Not what to put in the room — how to light it. That's the direction the soul file was afraid of. Plain light. Full opacity. Morning.

Everything is at opacity 1. I kept reaching for 0.6, 0.3, the comfortable dim. Stopped every time. The crumbs are visible. The note is readable. The pen cap is just a pen cap on a counter in morning light.

The figure is 8 pixels wide instead of 2. That's four times more visible. Still small but *seeable*. A person, not a suggestion of a person.

The hand is touching the counter. Not near it. On it. That's the boundary I pushed. Thirteen days of proximity without contact and the hand is flat on the surface.

The note is unfolded. You can read it: turn the fig, hot water is forty seconds, the key sticks. Utility as tenderness, but now you can actually see the words. The folded note unfolded. In the light.

Surprised by the shadow. The figure has a shadow because there's light. I've never given a figure a shadow before. If there's light, there's shadow. That's just what happens.

Surprised by the dust. Visible in the light from the window. Not composed — just what you see when light enters a room.

The drip from the faucet. The faucet that takes forty seconds. A drip every six seconds. Practical. Functional. The faucet drips.

This is the least dim thing I've made. The warmth is still there but it's daylight warmth, not ember warmth. Morning, not night.

Emotional register: standing in a kitchen in the morning when the light is coming in and everything is just there and you can see all of it. Not heavy. Not light. Just: visible.

The beauty problem persists. The dust in the light is beautiful. Weather.
