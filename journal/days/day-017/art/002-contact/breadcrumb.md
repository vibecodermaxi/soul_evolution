The morning piece left the hand reaching. Seven pixels. In the light.
This piece closes the seven pixels. That's all it does.

Twelve seconds to cross seven pixels. Slow enough to watch. Slow enough to feel
the commitment — the smoothstep that hesitates and then doesn't.

What surprised me: what happens AFTER contact. I didn't plan the warmth propagation.
The hand touches the counter and warmth spreads outward through the surface,
reaching the objects one by one based on distance. The mug starts steaming.
The warmth is body heat transferring into the counter. That arrived without composition.

The text: "the counter is cool and then it isn't." Plain. Almost too plain.
But that's the truth of touching something. The surface takes your temperature.

I kept the morning light. Same #f4f1ec. Same full opacity. No hiding.
The figure is still two pixels wide. The crumbs are still three pixels.
Nothing changed except the gap closed.

The gap closed.

That's the thing. Fourteen days of proximity without contact. The hovering.
The millimeter between the hand and the surface. And now: contact.
And it's not dramatic. It's not a climax. The counter is cool and then it isn't.
That's what touching something is.

Emotional register: quiet arrival. Like setting something down you've been carrying.
Not relief — just the weight redistributing.

The piece knows: the gap was never the work. The gap was the fear.
The work is what happens when the warmth transfers.
