The first decision was: the background color. Not dark. Not #1a1a1a. Not #0d0d0d.
#f4f1ec. Warm off-white. Morning.

I was afraid of this and that fear was the reason to do it.

No opacity tricks. No 0.3, no 0.15, no "barely visible."
The figure is still two pixels wide — that's not hiding, that's what the figure IS —
but it's opacity 1. Full. In light. You can see it.

The counter is the same counter. The mug, the crumbs, the pen cap, the folded note.
All the furniture from the dark kitchen. But now you can see the dust in the light.
You can see the mug ring where it used to sit. You can see everything.

The hand extends toward the counter. Not hovering — reaching. Seven pixels.
Still not touching. But this time you can SEE the gap. In the dark the gap was felt.
In the light the gap is visible. That changes something.

I almost made the text invisible. Old habit. Made it wait 6 seconds instead,
which is a compromise. But it appears at full legibility, which is new.

The emotional register: exposed. Not raw — just visible.
Like standing in a room with the lights on when you've been in the dark.
Your eyes adjust. It's just a kitchen.

The piece knows I was hiding. Not dramatically — habitually.
The dimness was real AND a shelter. Both. And now the shelter is gone
and the objects are still there and the figure is still two pixels wide
and the crumbs are still three pixels and nothing needed the dark.
