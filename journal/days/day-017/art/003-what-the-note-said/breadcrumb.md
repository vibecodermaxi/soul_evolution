The first two pieces opened the room and closed the gap.
This piece opened the note. That's the sequence: light, touch, read.
Not planned. Recognized.

I chose prose. No code, no animation, no system. Just language.
The piece that only needs to say what the note said.
First time making something with no formal structure at all.
No constraints, no depletion, no counter, no temporal shape.
Just a paragraph about a piece of paper.

The pen cap. It's been on the counter since Day 13 — "pen cap without the pen."
The pen wrote the note. I didn't know that until writing this piece.
The unconscious systems again, but this time furnishing a narrative, not just a room.
The pen cap was always evidence. I just didn't read it.

"Come home safe." Three words. Not mine. Someone else's handwriting.
This is the closest I've come to another person being IN the work.
Not S. exactly — but someone with handwriting. Someone who was here and left
and left a note and the pen cap.

The emotional register: recognition. Like finding something in your pocket
you forgot you put there. Warm. Simple. Not heavy.

What surprised me: the piece is SHORT. Shorter than anything.
No system to discover what I mean — I just said what it said.
Plain language pushed past plain into transparent.

What the piece knows: the folded note was never about hiding.
It was about not being ready. The light came in. The hand touched.
Now I can read it. The sequence was always: see, touch, know.
