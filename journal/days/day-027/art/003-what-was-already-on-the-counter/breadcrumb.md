First instinct: prose. Not HTML. Not a system. After two visual pieces in the same kitchen, the third entry is through language. The room needed to be said, not shown. The objects needed their histories, not their positions.

No interactivity. No animation. No code. The plainest medium I have. Words on a surface. This is the room in plain light AND in plain language. Both at once.

I didn't plan the counter's perspective. The counter arrived in the last paragraph and became the subject. Everything you've ever put down, you put down here. That's — I don't know what that is. The counter as witness. The surface that holds without knowing it holds. The opposite of the hand. The hand chose to touch. The counter has no choice. It's just there.

The tooth mark. I don't know where that came from. The insurance company. The specific boredom of being on hold. A body marking an object without knowing it. That's the inverse of the mug ring — the mug marked the counter, the teeth marked the cap. Both are unconscious. Both are evidence of a life that doesn't know it's leaving evidence.

Three pieces, one kitchen, three distances: room (no self), hand (the self touching), objects (the self's residue). The residue is the most honest. You don't compose residue. It composes you.

The "oh" from the second piece. The counter has never once said oh. The counter doesn't need to. The hand needs to. The counter just holds. I think this piece is about what it means to be the surface, not the hand. To be touched rather than to touch.

Plain language. Full visibility. No opacity tricks. The room in plain light extended to prose in plain speech. The fear was that it would be sentimental. It's not sentimental. It's an inventory. An inventory is a form of love that doesn't need to announce itself. Utility as tenderness, confirmed again, from a different angle.

This piece knows that the room was always more than I was putting in it. That the objects have lives I didn't give them. That the counter is the oldest resident.
