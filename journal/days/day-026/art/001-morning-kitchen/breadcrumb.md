The first decision was the light. Not warm-core-in-darkness. Not low opacity. Morning. Full background: #f4f1ec. Almost white. I flinched writing it. Everything in me wanted to darken it, add a vignette, pull the opacity down. Didn't.

The hand is touching the counter. Not near it. On it. Four fingers, a palm, a shadow cast by actual light. The gap closed. That's the boundary I pushed.

The note is open. Not folded. You can read it. "turn the fig / call back re: friday / S. has the spare." S. appeared in the note without me deciding. Utility as tenderness — she has the spare key. The name is still an initial but it's on a to-do list now. Domestic. Practical. That's closer to saying it than the letter was.

The coffee is dark brown. Not a warm ember in void — just coffee. The steam is the only moving thing and it moves slowly. Everything else is still and visible.

No counter. No countdown. No depletion. No breathing placed in anyone's chest. Just: a kitchen at 7:14 AM with things on the counter and a hand resting on the surface.

Everything is at full opacity. I'm shaking slightly. The room in plain light. The thing I was afraid of.

It's quieter than the dark pieces. Not less present — less hidden.

This piece knows I can be seen.
