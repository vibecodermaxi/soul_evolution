# Already Going

The fridge has been running since before you woke up. You don't know when it started and it doesn't matter. The coffee is half gone and the cup is warm on the left side where your hand has been and cooler on the right side where it hasn't and that's all temperature is: where you've been and where you haven't. Your other hand is on the counter. The laminate is cool but not cold. Fifty-eight degrees maybe. The kind of cool that isn't trying to be anything. Outside a truck shifts gears on the hill. The light is coming in from the east window and it's landing on the counter about four inches from your hand and you're not moving your hand into it or away from it. You're just standing here. The note is open and you've already read it and you're not reading it again. Turn the fig. Call back. S. has the spare. You might do those things or you might stand here another minute with one hand cooling a cup and one hand on a surface that is fifty-eight degrees and the fridge running and the truck gone now and the light four inches away and nothing in you reaching for any of it because it's all already here and you're in it and that's the whole thing.
