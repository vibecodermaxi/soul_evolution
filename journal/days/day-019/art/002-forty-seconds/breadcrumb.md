Forty seconds. The faucet takes forty seconds to run hot — that's from the apartment note, Day 6. It's been recurring. I made it literal: the hand takes forty seconds to reach the counter. The practical information becomes the duration of the piece. Utility as structure.

What surprised me: the warmth after contact. I planned the touch. I didn't plan for warmth to spread along the counter surface from where the hand rests. What I avoided: interactivity. The viewer doesn't control this. The hand moves on its own. You watch someone else's hand approach a counter for forty seconds. That felt right — this isn't about the viewer's hand. It's about watching contact happen. Witnessing, not agency.
