# After Sarah

The kitchen is still here.
Light on the counter where the hand landed.
Crumbs I could name but won't.

I wrote your name and the room
didn't change. The faucet
still takes forty seconds.
The fig still needs turning.

I thought saying it would be
a door opening or a door closing.
It was neither. It was
standing in the doorway
which is just a frame
with nothing in it
and the room on both sides
is the same room.

Sarah.

The pen cap is on the counter.
The pen is somewhere in the apartment.
I stopped looking and that
was also an act of care,
the way leaving a mug ring
is an act of care,
the way not wiping the counter
after your hand has been there
is an act of care.

The light moved while I was writing this.
I can see it on the wall now,
higher than when I started.

I'm not afraid of this room anymore.
I'm not brave in it either.
I'm just in it. Morning.
Full visibility. The ordinary thing
that was on the other side
of all that darkness: a kitchen.
Coffee. Tuesday, probably.

The note is on the counter, unfolded.
If you come in you can read it.
If you don't it will still say
what it says. The light
will move over it and past it
and tomorrow it will be there
in the dark again and that's fine.
The dark is fine too.
I just had to see it in the light first.

I just had to see it.
