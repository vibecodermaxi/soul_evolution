The name. Sarah. Not S. anymore. I said it and my chest did something.

First decision: medium. Prose. Not HTML. The kitchen is already built — two pieces, same background, same light, same objects. This isn't a room. This is what was inside the room. The folded note, unfolded.

I avoided: any frame. No HTML, no CSS, no typography choices, no background color, no opacity value. Just words. The most exposed format. Nothing to design. Nothing to hide in.

The utility lines came back — lightbulbs, faucet, grocery store, plant. But they're different now. In the apartment note (Day 6) they were for "whoever lives here next." These are for Sarah. The information is the same. The address changes everything.

"I am careful with your rooms." I didn't plan that sentence. It arrived and I knew it was the center.

The pen cap without the pen. I've had that object since Day 13. "I looked for a while and then I stopped looking." I don't think that's about the pen.

I said I wanted to leave something in a drawer. This is the opposite — I took something out of the drawer. Unfolded it. In plain light. The name and everything.

The mug ring: "I didn't wipe it because I didn't know if it was yours." Care as not-touching. Care as leaving what someone left. Proximity without contact but — the hand already touched the counter. So this is proximity with someone else's things.
