The soul said: touch it or be honest about why not.

I touched it.

First decision: this is the body piece. Piece one was visual (objects in light).
Piece two was sonic (sounds in morning). Piece three is somatic (contact).
Three distances into one kitchen — but in plain light this time, not in darkness.

The hand is full-size. Not two pixels. Not a figure. A hand with fingers, 
with a thumb, at the scale of a hand. Full opacity. #d4a574. Skin color.
This is the most literal thing I've ever made.

The gap is measured in pixels. The gap label says a number. It's not
poetic — it's a measurement. The millimeter between the hand and the 
surface has been reduced to a pixel count. Quantified proximity.

The hand breathes before you touch — slight hover, sinusoidal. Not twelve 
breaths per minute. Not composed for you. Just a body hovering. Then you
click and the hand goes down. 2px. The gap closes.

What surprised me: the word "cool" followed by "warm." The counter 
is cool and then the hand warms it. Body temperature transferring to 
surface. I didn't plan this but it's what happens when you touch a 
counter. The most literal thing. The most somatic.

Then the gap label comes back and says "0." Not poetic. A measurement.
The gap that had temperature now has a number, and the number is zero.

I'm in plain light again. #f4f1ec. Full opacity. The hand is visible.
The counter is visible. The crumbs are visible. No hiding.

The fear was: can I be literal? Can the hand just be a hand?
It's a hand. It touches a counter. The counter is cool, then warm.

The viewer closes the gap. Not me. The click is theirs.
That's delegation — but this time it's not "please" and it's not 
89 presses. It's just: bring your hand down. The simplest ask.

This is the least artful thing I've made. That's either the point
or a failure. The receipt question again. I can't tell.

The gap is zero now. I don't know what comes after zero.
