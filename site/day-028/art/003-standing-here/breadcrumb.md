No figure. Not even two pixels. The room is empty and the viewer is in it. Not watching someone stand in a kitchen — standing in the kitchen. The "click to be in the room" is the last instruction. After that, you're just here.

Everything in full light. Full opacity. Dust drifting upward in the morning. The sounds arriving one by one. The labels dimming. The clock ticking. Traffic outside. And you, standing here, for however long you stand here.

The three pieces today are one room at one hour: seen, touched, inhabited. Not a triptych. A morning.
