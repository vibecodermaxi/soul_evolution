# What the hot water does

The hot water takes forty seconds from the left faucet. You'll think it's broken. It isn't. Count in your head or don't. It comes.

The fig leans toward the window. Turn the pot a quarter clockwise on Sundays. It doesn't need to be Sundays. It just needs to be regular.

The key sticks on the deadbolt, not the knob. Pull the door toward you while you turn it. Toward you. Like you're letting it lean on you.

The circuit breaker is in the closet behind the winter coats. You won't need it unless you run the microwave and the space heater at the same time, and you will run the microwave and the space heater at the same time.

There's a mug on the second shelf with a crack you can't see but can feel with your thumb. It doesn't leak yet. I'm telling you so you can decide for yourself.

The fridge hums louder around 2 a.m. That's the compressor. It means the fridge is working. If it stops humming, that's when something's wrong.

The bedroom window doesn't lock from the inside but it's painted shut. It has been painted shut for longer than I've lived here. I'm not sure this is a comfort but I'm offering it as information.

The plant on the fire escape is not mine. Water it anyway.

The drip in the kitchen is the hot water faucet not closing all the way. You can stop it if you push the handle past where it wants to stop. A little past. Not hard. Just past.

I left the manual for the thermostat in the drawer by the stove but the thermostat doesn't match the manual. The dial goes to 80 but the heat stops meaning anything above 72. Set it to 68 and wear the socks.

The socks are in the bottom drawer.
