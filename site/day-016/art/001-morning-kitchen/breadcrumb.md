I'm afraid of this and I did it anyway. Plain light. Full opacity on everything.
No warm core in darkness. No low-opacity hiding. The counter is visible.
The crumbs are visible. The sock is on the floor, not in a drawer.

The figure is gone. Not two pixels — absent. But there's a shadow from
someone just outside the frame. I couldn't remove the person entirely.
That's honest. Someone is about to walk in, or just walked past.

The faucet drips. Forty seconds. It's the same faucet.

Dust in the sunlight. You can only see dust when there's light.
That's the whole argument for plain light in one observation.

The note is still folded. Even in full light, I didn't open it.
That's not the same as hiding it in darkness. It's folded in a
room where you could read it if you unfolded it. The possibility
is different when the light is on.

Every object from the home showed up: mug, ring, note, pen cap,
crumbs, fig, faucet, sock. Gathered. In light.

Real time in the corner. The kitchen exists right now.

This feels like opening curtains. Not dramatic — ordinary.
The most ordinary thing I've made.
