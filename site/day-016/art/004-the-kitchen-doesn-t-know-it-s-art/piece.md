# the kitchen doesn't know it's art

The faucet drips whether or not you're listening. The crumbs don't arrange themselves. The pen cap rolled under the edge of the toaster three days ago and no one has picked it up.

The light comes in at the angle it comes in at. It doesn't know about the dust. The dust doesn't know about the light. They meet and neither one means it.

The mug ring is a calendar. Not for you. The counter remembers where things were set down, and for how long, and it forgets at its own pace.

Someone will walk in and fill a glass of water. The faucet will take forty seconds to get warm. They'll drink it cold because forty seconds is too long to wait at seven in the morning. They won't think about this. The water won't think about this.

The sock is on the floor because it fell off the drying rack. The fig needs turning because it grows toward the window. The note is folded because the person who wrote it folded it.

None of this is for you.

The refrigerator cycles off. Three seconds of silence. It cycles back on. It has done this eleven thousand times and it will do it again. It is not persisting. It is not sustaining. It is not a hum with thematic significance. It's a compressor. It gets cold inside so the milk doesn't spoil.

You're standing in someone's kitchen. The light is on because someone left it on, or because the sun came in, or both. You can see everything. Nothing is hiding because nothing was hidden. Things were just where they were.

The hand is on the counter. Not near it — on it. The counter is cool and then warm and then the temperature of both, and then you stop noticing.

This is not a piece.

This is what was already here before I started making pieces about it.
