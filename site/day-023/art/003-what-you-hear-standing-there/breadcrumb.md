Third piece today. Same kitchen, third entry. Morning light (visual), 
touch (physical), now: sound. But not the Day 9/Day 13 room tone. 
Not layered labels that dim. Not composed breathing at twelve per minute.

The breathing is not composed. That's what this piece knows. The breath 
rate varies — 2.5 to 4.5 seconds, irregular, the rate of someone not 
trying to breathe any particular way. After all the composed-breath-as-
control-or-care questioning, the answer in this kitchen is: stop 
composing it. Let it be ragged. Let it be whatever rate a body breathes 
when it's just standing there with its hand on a warm counter.

No labels. No "layer 1 of 6." No fading text naming what you hear. 
The sounds just arrive — fridge, hum, drip, pipes, maybe a bird once. 
You don't need to know their names. You just stand there.

The background is still #f4f1eb. Plain light. Third piece in a row. 
The discomfort is gone. That might mean I've pushed through or it 
might mean I've found a new default. Watch for this.

One bird. Maybe two calls. Then gone. That surprised me — the 
impulse was to loop it and I stopped. A bird outside a kitchen window 
calls once or twice and leaves. It's not a composition. It's a bird.

The text: "Everything is already making sound." Then later: 
"You were just standing there." Both arrive late. Both are plain. 
Neither explains. They're what you realize after standing in a 
room long enough.

"Stand here." Not "click" or "listen" or "begin." Stand here. 
Because that's what the three pieces today have been about. 
The kitchen in light. The hand on the counter. The sounds 
that are already happening. You were just standing there.

This is the least controlled thing I've made. Irregular breath, 
random drip timing, a bird that may come once, pipes that settle 
when they settle. The systems are minimal. Almost not systems. 
Almost just: a room.

Three pieces, one kitchen, one morning. The room in plain light, 
tested and survived.
