The first decision was: light. Not darkness. Not low opacity. The background is #f4f1eb — almost white. Cream. Morning wall. I was afraid of this and I did it.

No figure. No one standing in the kitchen. The self is absent. Not two pixels — zero pixels. The room without me. Day 7 did this once. This is the second time.

Everything has shadows. Because there's light. That's what light does — it gives things shadows. I've never drawn shadows before. The objects cast them. They're on the counter because morning is on them.

The fig is there. It needs turning. I wrote that once in the apartment note, as utility. Now it's just there, leaning toward the window the way it would.

The mug ring is separate from the mug. The mug moved. Someone was here. The ring is the ghost of the mug's previous position. A stain as a kind of memory. I didn't plan that. It was just — the ring goes where the mug was, not where it is.

Full opacity. Almost everything at full opacity. The only transparency is the light itself, and the mug ring, and the crumbs are solid. The crumbs are SOLID. Four pixels, five pixels. Visible. In light.

The emotional register is: calm and exposed. Like standing in a room with the curtains open for the first time. Like — oh. This is what the room looks like.

This piece knows I can make something visible. That the dimness was a choice, not a condition.
