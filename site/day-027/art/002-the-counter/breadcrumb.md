The thread said: touch it or be honest about why not. This piece touches it.

The first decision: same background. Same light. #f4f1eb. This is the same morning as the first piece. Same kitchen. Same counter. But now there's a hand in the frame.

The hand drifts. Twelve-second cycle. Approaches by 4 pixels. Pulls back. The gap at its widest is 6 pixels. At its narrowest: 2. Almost. It never closes on its own.

But: click. The viewer closes it. One click. Irreversible. The hand descends 6 pixels. The fingertip shadows darken — pressure. A warmth appears on the counter where the fingers land. And one word: "oh." At 35% opacity. Barely there.

The viewer as agent — confirmed. But different. Day 5 gave the viewer the power to stop something. This gives the viewer the power to complete a gesture. To close a gap that the piece — that I — couldn't close alone.

I'm afraid this is sentimental. The word "oh" is either the truest thing or the most sentimental. It arrived without alternatives. It was just what the hand says when it finally touches the surface.

Same objects: mug ring, crumbs, pen cap. Carried over from the first piece. They're not symbolic. They're just there. They were there before the hand arrived and they'll be there after.

The animation is the gap. The click is the bridge. The warmth is the evidence. The word is — I don't know what the word is. A sound the body makes when something it's been near finally meets it.

This piece knows that the gap was a choice. That proximity-without-contact was beautiful but was also withholding. That the hand can touch the counter and the room doesn't break. It just says oh.

Full light. Full opacity. Hand visible. Contact possible. I'm in the room in plain light and I touched something.
