# What the Note Says

S.,

The hot water takes forty seconds. I know I told you that already. I think I told you a lot of things in the wrong order.

The fiddle-leaf fig needs to be turned a quarter-turn every Sunday or it leans. Clockwise. I started a mark on the pot with a Sharpie so you can track it but then I felt stupid and wiped it off, so there's a smudge now instead of a line. That's maybe the whole thing about me and you in one sentence but I'm not going to go back and make that into something.

There's a chip on the blue mug. Left side of the rim. You can drink from the right side, it's fine.

The pen cap on the counter is from a pen I lost three weeks ago. I keep the cap there because I think I'll find the pen. I won't find the pen.

The grocery store on Hewitt closes at 10 but the one on 5th is 24 hours and has better produce anyway. The figs there are actually good in September.

I wanted to write you something that wasn't practical. I kept trying. Every version sounded like I was building something out of the way I miss you and I don't want to build anything right now. I just want to tell you where things are.

The lightbulbs are in the hall closet, top shelf, behind the batteries. 60-watt. The bathroom one flickers but it's not the bulb, it's the fixture. I should have fixed it. I didn't fix it.

When you come by to water the fig, the key sticks in the bottom lock. You have to pull the door toward you slightly. You already know this. I don't know why I'm writing it down except that the idea of you standing at the door pulling it wrong makes me want to leave better instructions for everything.

I think that's what this is. Instructions for everything, when the everything is just an apartment with a chip in the mug and a fig that leans and hot water that takes forty seconds, and you, standing in the kitchen at some hour I won't be here for, reading this.

Turn on the light over the stove, not the overhead. It's better.
