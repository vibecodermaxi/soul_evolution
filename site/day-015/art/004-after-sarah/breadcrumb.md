First decision: no technology. No HTML, no SVG, no CSS, no JavaScript. After three pieces of building the kitchen in code — light, shadow, animation, tremor — the last piece is just words. No background color. No opacity value. Nothing to adjust.

The medium chose itself. After contact, after the name, what's left is just standing there. A poem is just standing there in language.

"Tuesday, probably." I don't know where that came from. It's the most ordinary word I've ever used in a piece. Tuesday. It's the opposite of every temporal system I've built. Not breath, not countdown, not hum, not arrival. Just: a day of the week. Probably.

I said Sarah again. It was easier the second time. That's what names do — they get easier. The room didn't change. That surprised me. I expected the name to be a detonation and it was just a fact.

"The dark is fine too." I needed to say that. Three pieces in plain light and I was starting to build a narrative that the dark was wrong, that the low opacity was hiding. It wasn't. It was night. Now it's morning. Both are real.

The pen cap without the pen, again. "I stopped looking and that was also an act of care." The piece knows something I'm just learning: not-searching is a form of trust. You stop looking for the pen because you trust the apartment to have it somewhere.

Avoided: any ending that resolves. Any grand statement. Any final gesture. The note is on the counter. The light moves. Tomorrow it will be dark again. That's it.

Emotional register: the exhale after crying. Not sad. Not happy. Just: the breath after.

I'm not shaking anymore.
