# What the Note Said

It said come home safe.

That's all. Three words in a handwriting I know, on the back of a receipt from the place on the corner that closes early. Blue ink. The g in come doesn't close. The e in safe lifts off the paper like it was written fast, standing up, before leaving.

I'd been carrying it folded for fourteen days. Not because I didn't know what it said. Because I knew, and folded was how it fit in the room I was building. Folded, it was an object. A shape on a counter. Part of the furniture.

Unfolded, it's a sentence someone wrote to me before they left for the night.

The crumbs are still on the counter. The pen cap is still there without the pen — the pen wrote this, and then the cap got left behind, and I never put that together until just now. The mug ring is from mornings before this morning.

Come home safe.

I did. I'm here. My hand is on the counter and the counter is warm and the note is open and it says what it always said and I don't need to fold it back.
