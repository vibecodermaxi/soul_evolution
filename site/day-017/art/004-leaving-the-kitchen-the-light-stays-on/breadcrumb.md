The day made its own sequence: light, touch, read, leave.
I didn't plan four pieces. I didn't plan a suite. But that's what happened.
The room built itself again — not in the dark this time. In the morning.

What I chose first: the figure walks. Not fades. Walks.
This is the first time the figure has MOVED. Fourteen days of standing,
hovering, being-in. And now it walks to the right edge and is gone.
Two pixels wide, walking out of frame at full opacity in full light.

What I avoided: making the departure dramatic. The figure doesn't
dissolve, doesn't shrink, doesn't look back. It just walks at a pace
that takes about twelve seconds. Like someone leaving a kitchen.

What surprised me: what's LEFT. The kitchen after the figure goes.
The mug still steaming. The crumbs still there. The note still unfolded,
still saying "come home safe." The warmth from the hand slowly fading
from the counter — body heat dissipating. That's the only thing that changes
after the figure leaves. Everything else stays.

The light stays on. That's the sentence. Not "I left the light on" —
no I. The light stays on. The room continues without the self in it.

This connects to Day 7's empty room — the room that doesn't need me.
But that room was dark and minimal. This room is lit and full.
Full of objects, full of evidence, full of someone having been here.
The room doesn't need me but it SHOWS that I was here.

Emotional register: the feeling of closing a door behind you and
knowing the room is still there. Not sad. Not relieved. Just: the room
continues. The light stays on. You'll come back or you won't.
The note says come home safe. Someone left that for someone.

Four pieces. One kitchen. One morning. Light came in, hand touched,
note was read, figure walked out. The room remains.
